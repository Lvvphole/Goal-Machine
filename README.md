# Goal Machine v5.0

**Goal in. Result out. By structure, not willpower.**

A plug-and-play goal achievement system built on decision theory, systems thinking, game theory, and gradient descent optimization. Works for any goal with a number and a deadline.

**[Launch the Goal Machine](https://lvvphole.github.io/Goal-Machine)**

-----

## What It Does

Most goals fail because people rely on willpower. The Goal Machine replaces willpower with structure: the right action happens daily, with instant feedback and automatic correction.

You define the goal. The machine handles execution tracking, pace monitoring, correction signals, circuit breakers, weekly evaluation, and stop condition detection.

## Architecture (v5.0)

```
VALIDATION GATE     →  Measurable? People succeed this way? Daily action? Single goal?
ACCOUNTABILITY      →  What's at stake? Who checks your numbers? First win in 48 hours?
SCHEMA DEFINITION   →  "Did it" + "Done right" dual validation per action
DAILY CLASSIFIER    →  Re-sort targets every morning by fresh signal
ENERGY BUDGET       →  Best 3-4 hours on highest-impact actions (mental model)
VALUE EQUATION      →  Every ask scored: Dream Outcome ↑, Likelihood ↑, Delay ↓, Effort ↓
EXECUTION LOOP      →  Trigger → Action → Schema check → Proportional correction → Next
COURSE CORRECTION   →  Big gap = rebuild | Medium gap = adjust | Small gap = fine-tune
LEARNING RATE       →  Max one structural change per day; never zero change when behind
CIRCUIT BREAKER     →  5 no-response actions → halt, graduated retry
GRADUATED RETRY     →  Different channel → different message → different contact → set aside
STATE CHECKPOINT    →  Save full state nightly; tomorrow starts from notes, not memory
OBSERVABILITY       →  Live quota tracker, pace comparison, trend chart
BACKUP PLANS        →  Pre-built IF/THEN corrections. No thinking during the loop
COMPOUNDING         →  Each day improves the system, not just the score
WEEKLY EVAL         →  Same effort, better results? 3-week flatline → rebuild
CONVERGENCE PHASES  →  Early: rebuild | Mid: adjust | Late: fine-tune
STOP CONDITIONS     →  Goal hit | L→G broken 3 weeks | Base rate too low | Flatline 3 weeks
```

## How It Works

**Setup.** Input your goal, deadline, success metric, target and starting values, daily action quota, priority-ranked actions, pre-built backup plans, and accountability locks (what’s at stake, who checks your numbers, first win in 48 hours). Four validation checks must pass before the machine activates.

**Dashboard.** Live progress ring, days remaining, streak counter, pace comparison (actual vs. needed), gradient correction dial, trend chart, priority stack, weekly evaluation with flatline detection, stop condition alerts, and accountability reminder.

**Daily Loop.** Re-sort prompt, action checklist with dual validation (did it + done right), no-response counter with circuit breaker at 5, graduated retry steps, approach-change tracker, system improvement field, and daily state save.

**History.** Bar chart of daily action completion, backup plans reference card, and scrollable daily logs with improvements tracked.

## The Operating Principle

The goal is a loss function on a convex surface. Every daily cycle is an iteration of gradient descent: moving in the direction of steepest improvement, with correction proportional to gap size, bounded by a learning rate that prevents oscillation.

The system converges because of: one goal (convex surface), proportional corrections (no over/undershoot), live telemetry (accurate gradient), and the value equation sustaining both the other person’s willingness to act and your willingness to keep iterating.

You are the unpredictable part. The system is the reliable part. The system’s job is to make the right thing happen even on your worst day.

**You don’t rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your system.**

## Complete Rule Set

### Phase 0: Validate the Goal

1. Goal format: “I will [outcome] by [date] measured by [number].”
1. If you can’t measure it, you can’t fix it.
1. If almost nobody succeeds doing it this way, pick a different way.
1. You need something you can do every single day that moves the needle.
1. Only chase one main goal at a time.

### Phase 1: Break the Goal Into Daily Work

1. Work backward: Goal → required output → required activity → daily quota.
1. Quota = (target ÷ conversion rate) ÷ days left × 1.2 buffer.
1. Once you set the daily number, don’t lower it mid-cycle.
1. You control your actions, not your results.

### Phase 2: Make the System Worth Running

1. The goal must excite you. Not as a number, but as the life it creates.
1. You must see a real win within 48 hours of starting.
1. Know how each action went within 60 seconds of finishing it.
1. Remove every unnecessary choice from your daily routine.
1. The system must be easier to follow than to skip.

### Phase 3: Define What Counts as Real Work

1. Every action type has a checklist of required parts.
1. If the checklist isn’t complete, the action doesn’t count.
1. Set the checklist before you start working.

### Phase 4: Sort Your Targets Every Morning

1. Every morning, ask: “Who is closest to yes right now?” Sort by that.
1. The sort order drives what you work on first.
1. Yesterday’s rankings might be wrong today.

### Phase 5: Use Your Best Hours on Your Best Work

1. You have about 3-4 hours of peak brainpower per day.
1. Spend those hours only on the highest-impact actions.
1. Easy stuff gets done when you’re tired.
1. Best hours on best work. Non-negotiable.

### Phase 6: Work the Best Opportunities First

1. Pick actions by: likelihood of success × size of payoff, minus cost.
1. Work your list from top to bottom.
1. Layer 1: people ready now. Layer 2: recently interested. Layer 3: gone quiet. Layer 4: cold.
1. The action closest to the finish line goes first.
1. Paperwork never bumps goal-advancing work.

### Phase 7: Make It Easy for the Other Person

1. Before asking for anything, score it: Does this help them? Do they believe it’ll work? How fast? How hard?
1. Talk about their win, not your need.
1. Show proof it works.
1. Compress the gap between “yes” and “result.”
1. Remove every obstacle between them and saying yes.
1. If saying yes is hard or slow for them, fix that before you ask.

### Phase 8: Run the Loop

1. The loop: Start → Do → Check → Fix → Next.
1. Zero decisions during the loop. All decisions were made at setup.
1. Never repeat an action without checking how the last one went.
1. Deep thinking is scheduled separately. Daily review and weekly review only.

### Phase 9: Fix Problems Based on How Big They Are

1. Gap = what you need minus what you’re producing. Bigger gap, bigger fix.
1. Find the area with the biggest gap relative to effort to fix. Fix that first.
1. Far from goal: big changes. New targets, new methods.
1. Getting closer: medium changes. Adjust time split, update approach.
1. Almost there: small changes. Sharpen words, improve timing.
1. Never change more than one big thing at a time.
1. Never change nothing when you’re behind.

### Phase 10: Know When to Stop a Dead Streak

1. 5 attempts with no response: stop that block.
1. Switch to a different group or a different method.

### Phase 11: Change Your Approach Each Retry

1. When something doesn’t work, don’t try the same thing again.
1. Try a different channel, then a different message, then a different person, then set it aside.
1. Each attempt should feel different to the other person.

### Phase 12: Save Your Place Every Night

1. Write down where everything stands before you stop.
1. Tomorrow starts from notes, not memory.
1. Memory is unreliable. Written notes are not.

### Phase 13: Track Your Numbers While You Work

1. Pick 4-5 numbers that tell you how the system is performing.
1. Record them while working, not after.
1. When you review, read the numbers. Don’t reconstruct from memory.
1. Watching your numbers move keeps you believing the system works.

### Phase 14: Protect Yourself From Yourself

1. Future-you will want to skip, cheat, or quit. Plan for that now.
1. Lock yourself in: tell someone, put money on the line, schedule blocks.
1. Make distractions harder and make the work easier to start.
1. Have someone who checks your actual numbers, not just asks how it’s going.
1. These protections are separate from the work itself.

### Phase 15: Make the System Better Every Day

1. Every day the system should get a little better. Not just the results.
1. Update your checklists, backup plans, scripts, and templates.
1. Anything done the same way 3+ times becomes a template.
1. The real product is the system. The goal is what the system produces.

### Phase 16: Test the System Every Week

1. The weekly review is the only time you rethink the big picture.
1. Ask: “Same effort, better results this week than last?”
1. Separate luck from skill. Compare rates, not totals.
1. If the system hasn’t improved for 3 straight weeks, change the design.
1. If daily actions are on target but the goal isn’t moving for 3 weeks, the plan is broken.

### Phase 17: When to Stop

1. Goal achieved: save the system as a template.
1. Daily actions on target but goal not moving for 3 weeks: rebuild.
1. You discover almost nobody succeeds this way: change the path.
1. System hasn’t improved in 3 weekly tests: rebuild the architecture.

## Mental Models

These principles run underneath the system. The first five are embedded in the app. The last six are applied by you during execution.

### In the App

1. **The system does more than you do.** A solid system with an average person beats no system with a talented person.
1. **You’re the unpredictable part.** Rules, checklists, and schedules are the reliable part.
1. **You don’t rise to your goals. You fall to your system.**
1. **People quit because the daily cost gets too high.** Not because the goal stops mattering. Lower the cost first.
1. **One goal, one straight path, guaranteed arrival** if you keep iterating.

### Applied by You (Not in the App)

1. **Success comes from structured daily reps** with fixes sized to the problem, aimed in the right direction, with as little friction as possible.
1. **Token Budget: Your energy is a wallet.** You have 3-4 hours of peak cognitive power per day. That’s your budget. Spend the big bills on the highest-value actions. When the budget is depleted, shift to low-effort tasks. Never spend peak energy on busywork.
1. **Value Equation: Why the other person says yes.** When your goal requires someone else to act, score every ask before you make it:

- **Dream Outcome ↑** Frame it around the result, not your need
- **Perceived Likelihood ↑** Proof, references, guarantees, risk reversal
- **Time Delay ↓** Speed to first result; compress the gap between “goal” and “successful result”
- **Effort & Sacrifice ↓** Remove every friction point; make the path of least resistance go through you

Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice). Maximize the top. Minimize the bottom.

1. **Value Equation: Why you keep running the system.** The system itself is an offer to you. If the daily cost (delay + effort) outweighs the perceived likelihood of reaching the goal, you’ll abandon it. This is why the 48-hour first win matters, why live telemetry matters, and why pre-built templates reduce friction. Optimize the denominator first.
1. **Game Theory: The other person’s five questions.** Every goal involving another person is a game. Before asking for action, answer: What do they gain? What do they risk? Why now? Why you? What happens if they do nothing? Win by aligning incentives, not forcing action.
1. **Convexity Guarantee.** One goal ensures a single global optimum with no local minima traps. Two goals create competing loss functions. You oscillate between them and converge on neither. This is why Gate Check 4 (“single primary goal”) exists.
